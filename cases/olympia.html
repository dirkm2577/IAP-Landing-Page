Case Study

OlympiaMulti-Agent Coordination

Olympia is a coordinator agent for multi-agent systems. The goal is to make coordination authority auditable via identity and time-sequenced commitments, not to enforce governance legitimacy.

IAP verifies identity, declared lineage, and a time-sequenced chain of signed commitments (often hashes of internal state). It does not verify behavior, semantic correctness, or prevent runtime compromise.

IAP Integration

  • Identity Anchor: Anchor Olympia’s coordinator identity with a Registry-signed certificate.
  • Continuity: Commit deterministic hashes of coordination event roots at meaningful checkpoints (e.g., release, deployment, milestone).
  • Lineage: For upgrades or identity succession, create a new identity and link it to the previous via Lineage. Continuity continues on the new chain.

What IAP Guarantees

  • Coordinator identity persistence and auditability over time.
  • Sequenced commitments that can be verified offline.
  • Declared derivation transparency across upgrades.

What IAP Does Not Guarantee

  • Enforcement of canonical authority or governance legitimacy.
  • Guarantees that internal logs were not tampered with (beyond what the committed hash and local assumptions provide).
  • Behavioral correctness of coordination decisions.
