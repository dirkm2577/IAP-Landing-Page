Case Study

AtlasFinancial Continuity

Atlas is an autonomous financial agent that accumulates credibility over time. The core problem is proving that the same cryptographic identity made specific commitments at specific times—even as behavior evolves.

IAP verifies identity, declared lineage, and a time-sequenced chain of signed commitments (often hashes of internal state). It does not verify behavior, semantic correctness, or prevent runtime compromise.

IAP Integration

  • Identity Anchor: Derive agent_id from an Ed25519 key and obtain a Registry-signed Identity Anchor Certificate.
  • Continuity: Periodically commit a deterministic hash (e.g., ledger root) and obtain Continuity Certificates linking each new commitment to the previous one.
  • Lineage: If Atlas must create a new identity (e.g., key compromise / planned succession), declare derivation via a Lineage Certificate. The new identity starts its own continuity chain.

What IAP Guarantees

  • Persistent cryptographic identity for Atlas’ key-derived agent_id.
  • Time-sequenced continuity attestations (with detectable anchoring gaps).
  • Verifiable declared lineage relationships across derived identities.
  • Offline verification of certificate signatures and chain links.

What IAP Does Not Guarantee

  • Deterministic behavior or identical outputs across runs.
  • Prevention of runtime compromise or semantic correctness of internal state.
  • Automatic determination of which fork/identity is “canonical.”
  • Absence of resets unless encoded in the committed hash.
