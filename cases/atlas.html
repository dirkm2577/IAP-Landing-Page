IAP-0.1 Identity Anchor Continuity Lineage

IAP verifies identity, declared lineage, and a time-sequenced chain of signed commitments (often hashes of internal state). It does not verify behavior, semantic correctness, or prevent runtime compromise.

Atlas Case Study

Scenario

Atlas is an autonomous financial agent that executes trades, manages

capital, and evolves its internal algorithms over time.

The key problem:

> How can external observers verify that the Atlas operating today is

> cryptographically continuous with the Atlas that operated yesterday?

Atlas' behavior is not deterministic. Its outputs depend on market data,

temperature settings, tool responses, and evolving internal state.

Therefore, IAP does not attempt to verify behavior.

IAP verifies **cryptographic identity and continuity of declared

commitments**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

IAP Integration

Step 1 --- Identity Anchor

Atlas generates an Ed25519 key pair. Its public key deterministically

derives its agent_id.

The IAP Registry issues an **Identity Anchor record**, attesting

that:

  • This public key existed at time T
  • The Registry signed this attestation
  • This was the origin identity of this chain

This is a cryptographic birth record --- not a behavioral

guarantee.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Step 2 --- Continuity Anchoring

Atlas maintains an internal ledger (e.g., AMCS). Periodically, it

computes a deterministic hash of its ledger root (memory_root).

It requests a **Continuity record** from the Registry, which

attests:

  • The same public key signed the request
  • The previous continuity record hash is referenced
  • The declared `memory_root` hash was committed at time T

Important:

IAP does not interpret the meaning of the hash. It only attests that the

identity declared this commitment at that time.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Step 3 --- Identity Succession (Key Compromise or Upgrade)

If Atlas must rotate keys:

  • A new key pair generates a new identity
  • A Lineage Certificate declares derivation from the previous identity

The new identity starts its own continuity chain. Observers can verify

declared lineage relationships.

IAP does not decide which chain is canonical. Observers evaluate lineage

and continuity themselves.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

What IAP Guarantees

  • Persistent cryptographic identity
  • Time-sequenced continuity attestations
  • Detectable anchoring gaps
  • Verifiable lineage declarations
  • Offline verification of signed records

What IAP Does Not Guarantee

  • Deterministic behavior
  • Prevention of runtime compromise
  • Internal semantic correctness of state
  • Absence of reset unless detectable via committed hash

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Result

Atlas becomes:

  • Persistently identifiable
  • Continuity-attested at chosen checkpoints
  • Structurally fork-distinguishable via lineage
  • Economically accountable at the identity level
