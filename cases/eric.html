Case Study

EricResearch Provenance

Eric is a long-running research agent. The goal is to provide verifiable provenance for checkpoint artifacts (datasets, outputs, model artifacts) over long horizons via signed, time-stamped commitments.

IAP verifies identity, declared lineage, and a time-sequenced chain of signed commitments (often hashes of internal state). It does not verify behavior, semantic correctness, or prevent runtime compromise.

IAP Integration

  • Identity Anchor: Establish a stable cryptographic identity for Eric with an Identity Anchor Certificate.
  • Continuity: At milestones, commit deterministic hashes of research artifacts and obtain Continuity Certificates linking each checkpoint to the prior one.
  • Lineage: If Eric is forked, a legitimate fork can declare lineage. Undeclared forks lack a verifiable lineage link.

What IAP Guarantees

  • Time-stamped provenance for checkpoint commitments by a consistent identity.
  • Tamper-evident continuity of declared checkpoint hashes.
  • Structural distinguishability of derived forks when lineage is declared.

What IAP Does Not Guarantee

  • Scientific correctness, completeness, or reproducibility by itself.
  • Behavioral sameness or identical outputs across runs.
  • Forcing a fork to declare lineage (malicious forks can refuse).
